M1 - Multiplying matrices

Example 1

Of the following three matrices, only two may be multiplied. \begin{align*} A= \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1 & -3 \\ -1 & 4 \\ 1 & -4 \end{array}\right] & & B= \left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & 6 & -4 & -4 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -1 \end{array}\right] & & C= \left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & 1 & 1 & -4 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -2 & -4 & 2 \end{array}\right] \\ \end{align*} Explain which two can be multiplied and why. Then show how to find their product.

Answer:

\[AB= \left[\begin{array}{cccc} 1 & 3 & -4 & -1 \\ -1 & -2 & 4 & 0 \\ 1 & 2 & -4 & 0 \end{array}\right] \]
